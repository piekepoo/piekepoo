Synesthesia



w/ Vera Williams and Su Polatoglu


Summary

Can we paint in different colours with our voices? A visitor can sing in the microphone and based on the produced volume, paint will drop on a spinning vinyl, causing the vinyl to be covered in paint. Paint-colour is dependent of the volume. Metal box covers the paint from splattering, making it an experience like a jukebox. Developed with Arduino.

Concept

Our initial idea was to create a machine that translates the singing voice of the user into an art painting. We recorded sound with a sensor, a microphone, which is converted into digital signal through voltages. The sound would be differentiated into three segments, low, medium and high volume. These volumes were linked to three different colours of paint, generating a different paint colour for each of the categories. The colours dripped on a rotating vinyl, creating an image through the dripping of different colours and through rotation. Our input was the sound recorded through the microphone and our output was the dripping colours on the canvas.

Motivation

We saw both singing and painting as a way to express yourself, so how cool would it be to combine them together?

Implementation

We used hardware, such as an Arduino, servo motor and a microphone. We hid the microphone in an actual 3D-pen-generated microphone so that it looked like as if you were singing in a paintbrush. The microphone was connected to an arduino, that controlled a servo motor that responded on 3 different volume levels. The servo motor opened 3 different holes in a bucket-lid. The bucket hold 3 tubes and therefore, turning of the hole in the lid decided which tube filled with paint would flow through the hole. We used glass so that the turning of the vinyl would still be visible. For the spinning vinyl we used a fan that we broke down and put together again to attach the vinyl to.
